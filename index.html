About Us



Peoria Community Against Violence, also known as PCAV, has been serving the city of Peoria since 2014, initially established as part of the Don't Shoot Initiative. For the first time since its inception, PCAV was able to hire paid employees in 2021. Since that time, we have focused on building credibilitywithin the community we serve by providing consistent support and services. Our target population iscommunity members directly affected by gun violence and other violent acts. This includes victims immediate family members, witnesses, and community members in areas that are ravaged by gun violence.

We have a nationally certified crisis response team that is called out by Peoria Police Department on homicides. Our team is specially trained to mitigate trauma, provide emotional support, and education on individual traumatic responses. We also work with Peoria Police Department (PPD) by providing case management services to recent victims of gun violence or move people out of the area quickly for their safety. We attend canvases where homicides occur with PPD and Greater Peoria Area Crimestoppers so that we can provide resources to communities recently affected by gun violence.

At PCAV, we provide on average, seven services per family as we address ALL of the social determinants correlated with gun violence. These factors include but are not limited to: poverty, housing instability, educational disparities, access to mental health services, and basic needs. We would not be able to provide the comprehensive services that we do, without our dynamic community partners. These organizations often fast track our clients based on severity of their situation, as the majority of our clients are in crisis when we meet them. Empirical studies show that increasing upward mobility for a family by just 25%, significantly reduces gun violence.

Services

PCAV provides a number of services and support systems for community members affected by gun violence. Read more below.

Crisis Response

Our Nationally Certified Crisis Response Team is a diverse group of dedicated community volunteers who provide emotional support at homicide scenes while assisting law enforcement, local hospitals and the Peoria County coroner's office to de-escalate emotionally charged situations. Our team has completed extensive training, including but not limited to: NOVA's basic 24 hour certification course, the FBI's death notification certification course, extensive reading of peer reviewed journals on trauma, underserved communities and vicarious trauma.

PCAV staff lead the crisis response team. They are also certified in NOVA's Advanced Crisis Response Training and FEMA's Basic Emergency Management.

Group Crisis Interventions

Group crisis interventions often take place at or near the site of the community trauma coincident within the first few days or week of the trauma event. This is the most commonly called upon service.
The technique allows response team members to address thoroughly all of the elements of the crisis intervention; to educate participants on the common pattern of crisis reactions and what long-term stress reactions are to be expected; and to help participants consider coping responses. The group sessions usually last between one and a half hours to 3 hours.

Group Defusing

A group defusing is usually conducted at or near the site of the community trauma coincident within the first few days or week of the event. Often the primary target populations for a defusing are emergency workers who face competing demands on their time, but short defusing sessions can be helpful to others as well.

Repetitive Group Intervention Protocol

The process of repetitive group intervention protocol is employed with a community perceives itself as being under siege as a result of numerous different disasters having taken place in a relatively short time period or where the ongoing trauma event (crime) has caused community members to live in fear or feel helpless.

Crisis Case Management

PCAV funds are used to provide survivors with emergency relocation and ongoing support. We assist with expenses such as temporary hotel relocation, deposit/1st month's rent, rental assistance, emergency food, clothing, or transportation, crisis intervention, crime victim compensation, and partner service referrals.

Material Provision

PCAV collects monetary and material donations to provide survivors with food, clothing, hygiene products, and more while they recover. Click the "Wish List" tab in our navigation bar to see what you could donate to help.
